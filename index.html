How I work with AI

AI is how I run the framework faster, not the framework itself

The process above predates my AI workflow. What changed is the speed of each step: I use Claude and similar tools to pressure-test reasoning before committing to it, to prototype in hours what used to take weeks (this site included), and to turn half-formed observations into something a stakeholder can react to the same day. The judgment calls stay mine. AI shortens the distance between noticing a problem and showing someone a fix.

Someone with the same experience who doesn't work this way arrives at similar insights slower, and usually keeps them in their head. Mine end up documented, testable, and shareable. The honest version of the differentiation: you could copy the tools in an afternoon. The judgment about which problem to solve is the part that took years.
